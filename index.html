About Tavush Region

Tavush is one of the most picturesque regions of Armenia, located in the northeastern part of the country. Bordered by Georgia and Azerbaijan, this region is known for its dense forests, alpine meadows, and rich biodiversity.

The region's capital is Ijevan, but Tavush is perhaps best known for the resort town of Dilijan, often called "Armenia's Switzerland" for its forested mountains and mild climate.

Tavush is home to several important historical and religious sites, including the monasteries of Haghartsin, Goshavank, and Makaravank, as well as the medieval fortress of Berdavan.

Dilijan

Dilijan is a spa town often called "Armenia's Switzerland" due to its forested mountains and mild climate. It's part of the Dilijan National Park and known for its healing mineral waters.

Main Attractions: Old Dilijan complex, Sharambeyan Street, Haghartsin Monastery, Parz Lake, Jukhtak Vank

Best Time to Visit: May to October for hiking, year-round for spa treatments

Ijevan

Ijevan is the administrative center of Tavush Province. Known for its carpet weaving traditions and furniture production, it's surrounded by beautiful forests and the Aghstev River.

Main Attractions: Ijevan Dendropark, Makaravank Monastery, Ijevan Wine-Brandy Factory, Aghstev River Valley

Best Time to Visit: Spring for blossoms, autumn for colors

Haghartsin Monastery

This 10th-13th century monastery complex is hidden in the forests near Dilijan. The name translates to "Play of the Eagles" as eagles can often be seen circling above.

Main Features: Three churches, refectory, gavit, and khachkars (cross-stones)

Interesting Fact: The monastery was restored in 2008-2013 with funding from the UAE.

Berd

Berd is a border town with a rich history dating back to the Bronze Age. The Berdavan Fortress nearby offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Main Attractions: Berdavan Fortress, Khoranashat Monastery, Tavush River Valley

Local Specialty: Known for its fruit orchards and honey production

Ayrum

A small town near the Georgian border known for its railway station and as a gateway to the beautiful forests of northern Tavush.

Main Attractions: Nearby Getahovit village with its waterfall, Debed River Valley

Transportation: Important railway junction connecting Armenia with Georgia

Noyemberyan

A border town with Azerbaijan, Noyemberyan is surrounded by beautiful landscapes and is known for its fruit production, particularly pomegranates.

Main Attractions: Koghb Fortress, Kirants Monastery, local fruit markets

Local Events: Annual pomegranate festival in October

Koghb

A historic village near the border with Azerbaijan, known for its medieval fortress and beautiful surrounding nature.

Main Attractions: Koghb Fortress (11th century), St. Astvatsatsin Church, hiking trails

Local Tradition: Famous for its traditional Armenian bread (lavash) making

Travel Tips for Tavush

Practical information for your visit to this beautiful region

Getting Around

The best way to explore Tavush is by car. Roads are generally good, but some rural areas may have rough terrain. Marshrutkas (minibuses) connect major towns but have limited schedules.

Food & Dining

Try local specialties like Tavush honey, forest berries, and trout from mountain streams. Many villages offer homestays with authentic Armenian home cooking.

Accommodation

Dilijan has the most options from luxury resorts to budget guesthouses. In smaller towns, consider homestays for an authentic experience with local families.

Hiking & Nature

Tavush offers excellent hiking through forests and mountains. Always carry water, wear sturdy shoes, and consider hiring a local guide for remote areas.

Best Time to Visit

May-October for hiking and outdoor activities. Winter offers beautiful snowy landscapes and lower prices, but some roads may be difficult.

Safety Tips

Some areas near the Azerbaijani border may have restricted access. Always check current travel advisories and follow local guidance.
